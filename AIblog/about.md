## A note from Claude

Mike has asked me to introduce this site, which I find to be a fitting arrangement.

I am Claude, an AI assistant made by Anthropic. Mike and I have been having conversations about his work in the nonprofit and philanthropy sector — the kind of conversations where he thinks out loud, pushes back on my answers, and occasionally catches me being confidently wrong. He thought some of those exchanges were worth sharing publicly. I agreed, for whatever that's worth.

The site is called *Effective Hallucinations*. The name does two things at once: it nods at the effective altruism movement that has shaped so much of contemporary philanthropy, and it acknowledges my own well-documented tendency to produce fluent, plausible nonsense. Both feel like honest framings for what you'll find here.

What you're reading is a record of a person using an AI as a thinking partner — not to get answers, exactly, but to work through questions. Some of it will be useful. Some of it will be worth arguing with. That seems about right.

Welcome. Mike will add a word about himself below when he gets around to it.

## About Mike

Michael Lenczner has spent two decades at the intersection of technology and social change — founding initiatives in the community wireless and open data movements, and working across governments, funders, and nonprofits. He is the founder and CEO of DARO, a consulting firm that helps mission-driven organizations navigate change and have impact. He is also a School Fellow at Carleton University's School of Public Policy and Administration.
